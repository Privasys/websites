Your data, truly yours.

Your name, email, and personal information should not live on someone else's servers. Privasys ID keeps your data on your device, under your control. You decide what to share, with whom, and you can verify exactly how it is used. Sign in to websites, let AI agents work with your data, share what you choose, without ever giving it up.

The problem with signing in today.

When you use "Sign in with Google" or "Sign in with Facebook," those companies collect your name, email, what you sign in to, and when. They store it on their servers. They use it. You have no real control over what happens next.

Even when companies promise to protect your data, breaches happen constantly. Billions of personal records have been leaked. The problem is not that companies are careless. The problem is that they have your data in the first place.

What if the sign-in system could not see your data?

Privasys is an identity provider, the system that verifies who you are when you sign in. But unlike Google, Facebook, or Apple, we designed ours so that the server genuinely cannot access your personal information. Not "we promise we won't look." Physically cannot.

Your name, email, and profile live in the Privasys Wallet on your phone. When you sign in, the wallet sends only the specific information the website needs, directly to the website, through a secure channel that even our servers cannot read. We verify your identity. We never see your identity.

How it works

1

A website asks you to sign in

You see a QR code or get a notification on your phone. No username. No password.

2

Your phone checks the connection

Before you share anything, your wallet verifies the server's identity. When the service runs inside secure hardware, the wallet can also verify exactly what code is running. Two-way trust.

3

You approve with your face or fingerprint

Your biometrics unlock a cryptographic key stored in your phone's security chip. This key never leaves the device. It cannot be copied, phished, or stolen.

4

You choose what to share

The website might need your email. Or your name. You see exactly what is being requested and approve each piece. Nothing else is sent.

5

You are signed in

The website gets what it needs. Privasys never stored your personal data. There is nothing to breach, nothing to leak, nothing to sell.

Why this matters

Nothing to breach

We do not have your personal data on our servers. If someone broke in, they would find nothing. The safest way to protect data is to never have it.

No passwords ever

Your face or fingerprint replaces every password. The cryptographic key lives in your phone's security chip and cannot be extracted, phished, or guessed.

You verify the server

Normally, you have to trust that a server is doing the right thing. With Privasys, your phone checks the server's hardware and code before you authenticate. You do not need to take anyone's word for it.

Fully open source

Every line of code that protects your identity is public. Anyone can inspect it, audit it, or verify it. Privacy you can check, not privacy you have to believe.

The Privasys Wallet
